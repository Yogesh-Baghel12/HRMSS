Company Policies

  1. Purpose

    The purpose of this leave policy is to establish guidelines for requesting, approving, and managing employee leaves to ensure consistency, fairness, and smooth operations within Spiral Mantra Pvt Ltd.

    Employees are required to apply for leaves by writing to their respective manager and must copy “hr@spiral-mantra.com”.

    Note: Responsibility for applying and making sure the same is approved by the Manager lies solely with the employee. The employee is required to apply or inform for Planned Leave minimum 2 days prior to the date he/she wants to take the leave.

  2. Types of Leave

    Spiral Mantra Pvt Ltd provides the following types of leave to its employees:

    Planned Leave --- 1 for every month completed

    All Regular employees are entitled for Paid Planned Leave of 1 day for every completed month. Employees joining prior to 15th of the month will be given a credit of 1 day of planned leave in the same month.

    Unplanned Leave --- 0.5 day for every month completed

    Employees will be entitled for 0.5 Unplanned Leave for every completed month with the maximum unplanned leave balance not exceeding 6 at any point of time.

    Maternity Leave --- 26 Weeks

    All regular women employees who have completed 120 working days during the twelve months immediately preceding the date of her expected delivery are entitled to avail paid Maternity leave. Maternity leave is for a period of 26 weeks in continuation. The application for maternity leave has to be made at least seven weeks before proceeding on leave. In case required, leaves can be taken Pre-Delivery, for a maximum of 6 weeks. Entitled to six weeks of leave in the case of miscarriage.

    In case of illness arising out of pregnancy, an additional one-month leave can be availed on doctor’s certification. Such leaves should be considered as Leave without Pay and shall be governed as per Clause 4.3.6 of the Leave Policy.

    The benefit of Paid Maternity leave shall be 12 weeks if you have more than two children.

    Paternity Leave --- 5 Days

    Fathers of newborn children or adopted children will be entitled to 5 days of paid leave. This leave has to be availed within two weeks of the birth of the child or adoption as the case may be. Half day leaves cannot be availed under this category of leaves.

    Leave Without Pay

    Leave without pay is allowed maximum up to 1 month with prior approval from his/her Manager. Leave without pay would be given as a payroll input and the salary deduction would be done accordingly. In the case of LWP, intervening weekly offs are included while calculating total no. of leave days.

    Hospitalization Leave – 10 Days

    All such employees requiring self-Hospitalization due to exigencies like sickness & accident are allowed 10 days of paid leaves under Hospitalization Leave subject to submission of Medical Certificate, Admission & Discharge Summary details to HR Department. All intervening weekly offs & holidays would be counted to calculate the total number of Hospitalization leaves. Leaves exceeding 10 days of hospitalization need to be submitted under the category of Planned & Unplanned Leaves and rest leaves should be applied as Leave without Pay by the employee.

    Accumulation and Encashment of Leaves

    Employees can accumulate Planned and/or Unplanned leaves. Firstly, 10 leaves out of the total leaves accumulated shall be carried forward as planned leave to the next year. The remaining leaves (up to 12 days in a year) shall be encashed on the basis of the average basic component of the CTC. Any leaves remaining after carrying forward and encashment shall lapse.

    Employees whose employment ends before completion of one year of service with Spiral Mantra are not eligible for Leave Encashment.

    Leaves during Probation Period

    The employee shall be on probation for the first six months from the date of joining unless notified otherwise. During this period the employee shall be granted the regular amount of leaves i.e. 1 planned and 0.5 unplanned per month. The employees on probation must not have any negative leave balance. All negative leaves in the probation period shall be treated as leave without pay.

    Leaves during Notice Period

    An employee is not entitled to avail any leave type while he/she is serving the notice period. In case an employee chooses to take leaves during the notice period, he/she shall be required to take prior approval of the manager. The notice period shall be extended by the number of days for which the employee is on leave.

    Notes:

    • Half day leaves can also be availed and applied for.

    • Leaves shall be credited in an individual's account on a monthly basis. On the first day of the month 1 Planned Leave & 0.5 Unplanned leave shall be credited. If an employee joins before the 15th of a month, then that month shall be included for the purpose of crediting Leave Balance.

    • At any point of time, employees can avail current leave balance + 5 advance leaves but total no. of leaves should not exceed more than 10 working days.

    • Provision of Advance leave is available to cater to exigencies in the family, extended medical problems beyond the hospitalization leave, marriage leave etc. Advance leaves shall only be granted subject to approval from the manager and above keeping in mind the following points.

    • The impact it would create on a project due to an individual's absence from work.

    • Manager has the discretion to disapprove advance leaves.

    • Any advance leave availed by the employee will be adjusted from the future credit of leave balance. In case an employee leaves the organization before squaring off the advance leaves, excess leaves will be recovered in full & final settlement of the employee.

    • The maximum leave entitlement one can have is 22 and the minimum being 0. Any leave balance above 22 shall lapse automatically.

    • The employee is entitled to 12 days of paid leaves in a year and 6 days of Casual leave. In addition, the employee will have 10 public holidays mentioned under the leave policy of the employee.

    • The Employees need to apply for a planned leave on the Application form.

    Leave Approval Process

    Employees are required to submit leave requests through the company's designated leave management system or to their immediate supervisors. Leave requests should be submitted well in advance whenever possible, especially for planned absences.

    Supervisors are responsible for reviewing and approving leave requests in accordance with operational requirements and staffing needs. Employees will be notified of the approval or denial of their leave requests in a timely manner.

    Leave Approval Process

    Annual leave accrues based on the length of service and is typically earned on a monthly basis. Accrued leave can be utilized once it has been earned, subject to supervisor approval and operational requirements.

    Unused annual leave may be carried over to the following year up to a certain limit, as determined by company policy. Any accrued but unused leave will be paid out to employees upon separation from the company, in accordance with applicable labor laws.

    Reporting and Recording

    Employees are responsible for accurately recording their leave usage in the company's leave management system or designated forms. Any discrepancies or errors should be reported to the HR department for correction.

    Policy Compliance

    All employees are expected to familiarize themselves with and comply with this leave policy. Failure to adhere to the policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

    Policy Review

    This leave policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure its effectiveness and compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

Attendance policy

Objective: The objective behind attendance policy is to encourage attendance and to monitor employees regarding their punctuality.


Purpose: The attendance policy of Spiral Mantra Pvt Ltd is designed to ensure that all employees understand the expectations regarding attendance and punctuality, thereby promoting a productive and efficient work environment. The purpose of the attendance policy is to reduce absenteeism in the workplace thereby implementing and enforcing an attendance policy.

• Spiral Mantra understands and believes in work life balance, we are dedicated to creating a healthy and flexible work environment for our employees.

• Team members are required to complete a minimum 9 Hrs each day and 5:30 Hrs in half a day.

• Team Members can avail 2 shorts leaves where he/she must complete minimum 7 hrs, failing to do this may result in half day leave.

• In case an employee is unable to attend office due to some emergency and it is part of unplanned absence as per attendance policy in office. The immediate manager should be informed on the same by telephone, message or email. In case the immediate manager is not reachable, HR should be informed on the same. The employee must call within 1 hour of starting work timings.

• The employees have to login through a punching machine and login form or report to work according to the project assigned or requested by the manager.

• At the end of every month, monthly attendance is shared with all the team members of Spiral Mantra and their respective manager.

• For any discrepancy, the respective employee can revert within 2 days after sharing the attendance.

• The normal office timings will be as follows:

 - Monday to Friday – 9 hrs (Flexible timing).

 - Lunch break: 45 minutes.

 - Saturdays and Sundays are off days.


Importance of having Attendance Policy:

1. Makes employees more regular and punctual.

2. Increase productivity.

3. Better use of skilled resources as the most skilled resource is of less use in case they are not present regularly.

4. Increases employee reputation for dependability.

5. Helps management to take labor related decisions.

6. Reduces absenteeism.

7. Major attendance issues can be identified.

8. Good attendance helps you in getting a good position.

9. Poor attendance puts a responsible manager or co-worker under pressure to carry out the job of others besides their own job.

10. Good attendance record increases morale.

11. Attendance policy makes sure that employees are paid in full and on time.

12. Helps in making decisions on how attendance can be improved.

Why is it necessary to complete working hours?

Completing working hours is necessary for several reasons:


1. Operational Efficiency: A consistent presence ensures that teams can function smoothly without disruptions. When employees complete their designated working hours, it minimizes the risk of delays in project timelines or services.

2. Resource Allocation: Completing working hours ensures that these resource allocations remain accurate, preventing under or overstaffing situations.

3. Fairness and Equity: An attendance policy ensures that all employees are held to the same standards regarding their presence and availability. This promotes a fair work environment where everyone contributes their fair share to the company's goals.

4. Service: Completing working hours ensures that there is adequate coverage to serve clients during regular business hours. This is crucial for maintaining client satisfaction and loyalty.

5. Legal Compliance: In some jurisdictions, labor laws may require companies to track and monitor employee working hours for compliance purposes. Adhering to these regulations helps prevent potential legal issues for the company.

6. Performance Management: Attendance is often linked to performance evaluations. Completing working hours demonstrates reliability and commitment, which can positively impact performance assessments and career advancement opportunities.

7. Team Collaboration: Consistent attendance fosters better collaboration among team members, as everyone is available during regular working hours for meetings, discussions, and coordination.

In summary, completing working hours as outlined in an attendance policy is essential for maintaining operational efficiency, ensuring fairness, complying with regulations, and fostering a positive work environment.

Spiral Mantra believes in providing healthy working hours to the employees. We comply with the legal provisions of working 9 hours a day. It is a two-way effort, and hence the employees are required to reach the workplace on time.

This policy establishes guidelines for governing the hours of work and attendance for all employees of the company. Employees are obligated to comply with the company’s working time as a mandatory requirement of employment.

The policy is applicable to all employees of the company.

Attendance Expectations:

1. Regular Attendance: All employees are expected to report to work on time as scheduled. Punctuality is crucial for the smooth functioning of our operations.

2. Notification of Absences: In the event of being unable to attend work due to illness, personal reasons, or emergencies, employees must inform their immediate supervisor or the HR department as soon as possible, the immediate supervisor should be informed on the same by telephone, message or email preferably before the start of their scheduled shift.

3. Attendance Records: Accurate records of employee attendance, including absences and tardiness, will be maintained by the HR department.

Consequences of Non-Compliance:

1. Verbal Notification: Upon the first instance of non-compliance with the attendance policy, employees will receive a verbal notification.

2. Email Notification: : Continued violations may result in a written notification, detailing the areas of concern and the expected improvement.

3. Warning: If attendance issues persist, employees will be warned and their attendance will be closely monitored.

4. Absconded: In case an employee is absent for more than 5 days without prior notice or approval. In such cases, it will be considered as job abandonment. The employee is then separated from employment as a voluntary quit.

Review and Amendments:

This attendance policy is subject to review and amendment as necessary. Any changes will be communicated to all employees in a timely manner.

Appraisal Policy

1. Purpose

• Be a positive process.

• Raise the quality of services provided by motivation.

• Increase job satisfaction.

• Identify appropriate training and development requirements.

Policy aims: Spiral Mantra recognises that employees perform most effectively when they have clear expectations of their job role and purpose, their own targets or objectives.

The policy aims to ensure that employees:

• Know what is expected of them i.e. the required standard of performance and how they should do their jobs.

• Receive feedback which aims to improve and develop performance and recognise their achievements.

• Identify areas where improvement is required and training and development needs.

• Have a mutually agreed plan to achieve both development goals and employee career development.

Roles and Responsibilities:

Spiral Mantra is responsible for:

• Setting sustainable standards.

• Providing advice and guidance to managers on implementing the policy.

• Ensuring that appropriate training and development is available for managers and employees.

• Holding an appraisal meeting and an appraisal review each year.

• Appraising employees in a fair and objective manner against agreed objectives and action plans.

• Following up actions arising from appraisals.

• Ensuring that a written record of the appraisal meetings is completed.

• Informing employees how the appraisal scheme works and how it will affect them.

Managers are responsible for:

• Ensuring each member of staff in their team is clear about what is expected of them.

• Ensuring that new employees have work targets and objectives set as part of induction.

• Meeting employees on a regular basis to review progress.

Employees are responsible for:

• Taking an active role in reviewing their performance appraisal and target setting.

2. Appraisal Cycle

The performance appraisal cycle will be conducted annually, with reviews typically taking place at the end of the financial year. However, additional performance discussions may be held throughout the year as needed.

3. Participants

All employees of Spiral Mantra Pvt Ltd, regardless of position or department, are subject to the performance appraisal process. This includes full-time, part-time employees.

4. Evaluation Criteria

Performance evaluations will be based on a combination of factors, including:

5. Performance Ratings Employees will be rated on a scale from 1 to 5, with 1 being unsatisfactory and 5 being exceptional. Ratings will be assigned based on a comprehensive review of performance throughout the appraisal period.

• Achievement of goals and objectives

• Productivity and efficiency

• Communication skills

• Teamwork and collaboration

• Leadership abilities (where applicable)

• Attendance and punctuality

5. Performance Ratings

Employees will be rated on a scale from 1 to 5, with 1 being unsatisfactory and 5 being exceptional. Ratings will be assigned based on a comprehensive review of performance throughout the appraisal period.

6. Appraisal Process

The performance appraisal process will involve the following steps:

•Self-assessment: Employees must be informed of the appraisal at least 4 weeks before the appraisal one-to-one meeting. A copy of the appraisal self-assessment form should be given to the employee so the employee has an opportunity to contribute. A copy of the completed self-assessment form should be returned to the manager 1 week before the appraisal one-to-one meeting.

• Appraisal Performance: The manager will use the appraisal performance checklist to prepare for an appraisal meeting by considering what criteria to use to measure employee performance this will set the measure for performance over the next year.

• The one to one meeting: The appraisal one-to-one meeting will be carried out in private so as to provide for confidentiality. Sufficient time will be allocated so that the meeting is unhurried and any discussion is properly considered. However, it must be understood by the employee and the manager that information discussed in an appraisal may need to be disclosed to others. The one-to-one form should be used to record an employee / manager meeting. The manager should complete this form at, or immediately following the meeting, ensuring that it is signed by both themselves and the employee. A copy will be given to the employee.

• The Appraisal Record: When an appraisal is completed, it should be recorded using the appraisal form. This form should be completed by the appraiser and countersigned by the employee and the appraiser’s manager so the employee is aware of the competencies that are critical for effective performance of that role. A copy of the form should be given to the employee as soon as possible.

7. Performance Recognition

Outstanding performance will be recognized and rewarded through various means, including:

• Merit increases: A merit increase is a pay raise given to an employee based on their performance. This is often used as an incentive to motivate employees in the workplace by recognizing their hard work.

• Promotion opportunities: Our appraisal policy outlines clear criteria for promotions, focusing on consistent high performance, relevant experience, skill development, leadership qualities, and alignment with company needs. We support employee development through training, mentorship, and career pathing, ensuring a transparent and fair approach to career advancement.

• Employee recognition programs: Our Employee Recognition Programs are designed to acknowledge and reward outstanding contributions and achievements, fostering a positive and motivating workplace culture. Key components include:

1. Spot Awards: Immediate recognition for exceptional performance or significant contributions.

2. Employee of the Month/Quarter: Regularly honoring individuals who demonstrate excellence and embody company values.

3. Peer-to-Peer Recognition: Enabling employees to recognize and appreciate their colleagues’ efforts through a structured platform.

4. Service Awards: Celebrating milestones such as work anniversaries to honor long-term commitment.

5. Achievement Awards: Recognizing significant achievements such as project completions, innovation.

9. Appeals Process

Employees who disagree with their performance appraisal rating may have the opportunity to appeal the decision through a formal appeals process outlined in the company's HR policies.

10. Policy Review

This performance appraisal policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure its effectiveness and relevance to the company's goals and objectives.

11. Compliance

All managers and employees are expected to adhere to this performance appraisal policy and cooperate fully in the appraisal process.
